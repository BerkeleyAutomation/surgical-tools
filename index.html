Autonomously Replaceable Surgical Tools

Interchangeable Instrument Mount and Precision Needle Injection System

View project on GitHub

This is a project page for Autonomous Surgical Tumor Resection with an Interchangeable Instrument Mount and Precision Needle Injection System.

Abstract

Supervised automation of selected sub-tasks in Robot-Assisted Minimally Invasive Surgery (RMIS) has potential to reduce surgeon fatigue and operating times. We present two novel devices to facilitate automation. The first is a low-cost 3d-printed single-use instrument mount that uses the gripping action of a standard RMIS instrument to securely hold and manipulate a range of standard instruments such as scalpels and novel instruments such as palpation probes. The second is a needle injection system designed to facilitate precision injection of chemotherapies, stem cells, adhesives, and other fluidic material to specific internal targets. The single-use needle is attached using the interchangeable instrument mount and the fluid flow through the needle is controlled via an externally-mounted automated lead screw. To explore how the mounting and injection system perform, we combined them with a palpation probe and scalpel to automate tumor resection: a common multi-step surgical procedure to locate, expose, and debride a subcutaneous tumor and seal the resulting wound with adhesive. This paper reports details on the design, implementation, and initial experiments with custom phantom tissues.
